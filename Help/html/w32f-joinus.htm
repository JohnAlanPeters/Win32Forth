Who are we / How to join us

Who's the We, Who's the I ?

The Win32Forth project group was formed in 2002 as a loose affiliation of programmers and users of Tom Zimmer's Win32Forth system. Tom interests took him elsewhere in 2001 and 2002 (mainly Java), and he no longer took an active interest in his system. Tom gifted his product  into the public domain; this group intends furthering the work of maintaining and developing a Forth product suitable for Windows and the 21st century based on his work.

Some of this documentation is based on Tom's original work; when you see "we" it means the Win32Forth group; when you see I, it's Tom speaking. You is definitely not us or him.

How to join us ?

The simple way is to join the Win32Forth Forum (also called the Win32Forth Yahoo Group) at http://groups.yahoo.com/group/win32forth/ . There, you can share information, files, ask for questions etc. You just need to create a new yahoo email and register to the group : join by subscribing at win32forth@yahoogroups.com .

 

If you want to get more involved in Win32Forth, and contribute to its development, then explore the Win32Forth Project group at http://win32forth.sourceforge.net . What you will find here is dowloads, another mailing list, bug reports, how to become a Win32Forth developper and to enter the CVS (Concurrent Version Software).

Specially for developpers, this page explains how to use the CSV.

 

Here is the somewhat original way the CVS runs :

[ You have to remember that this is a collaboration between people who have never ever met, except here; many have full-time jobs, some are retired, others -- well, who knows or cares what they are or do?

To use W32F, you download it, for free. If it doesn't work for you, then ask here; collectively and individually, we'll get you up and running.

No-one gets paid, and the only qualification to become a developer is to submit code. If your code has some utility, it will get added to the CVS. Based on your code or documentation submitted, we'll take a consensus decision (we do most things by consensus, and the 'we' are the current developers and users. Do you know, right now, I couldn't tell who is a developer or isn't, and I've been involved and developing W32F since 2003?)

But we set no timescales, we have no expectations of each other, there's no criticism for failing and much praise for succeeding. We do what we can when we can, because of a common interest in Forth. This is not a commercial development, or a normal open source development with committees and lead developers and hierarchies.

This is a very special place. ] - Alex McDonald -
